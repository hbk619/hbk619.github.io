As a student I heard the terms "client", "server", "DNS" and "cache" a lot on my placement I used a VNC client to connect to servers and worked on a web app and still didn't really understand, until I saw the physical server in a room and realised the "client" was the thing on my machine and server was some other machine somewhere else. Had a kind-hearted soul created some analogies for me I might have understood faster, so here's one for your entertainment.

We join Dave when he has just been told by his friend "Mate, there's this sick place that cooks your food and delivers. It's called Pizza Hut!".

{%- include comic.html -%}

Notice how Dave got the number for Pizza Hut. He could have asked literally everyone he know or strangers on the street but that would take a long time! Instead, there's this useful thing called a "phone book" that lists all companies with their phone numbers (like a physical contact list...).

Once he's got that he can call them and place his order, he is the client, making a request to the server (Pizza Hut). He waits a while and then a pizza arrives! His request has been fulfilled on the server side (Pizza hut cooked his pizza).

Having the number ready for the next order is the "cache", like remembering something rather than looking it up again. If Dave can remember the number when he next wants pizza it'll be faster to order, rather than looking in the phone book every time. Much like how if the client remembers where it found the server, the next request will be quicker as it doesn't need to ask the DNS server.
